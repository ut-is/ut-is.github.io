Talk

Education and Research in Computer Science and Computational Science

Speaker: Yoshihide Yoshimoto (Graduate School of Information Science and Technology, The University of Tokyo)

Wednesday, December 15, 2021, 15:00-16:30

Zoom

Abstracts：

In the early days of computing, one of the primary motivations for its development was its application to scientific and technical computing. The exponential development of computing since then has greatly benefited science and technology.

The speaker's teaching and research activities in the Department of Computer Science and Information Science cross the boundaries between computer science, computational science, and condensed matter physics, and two topics will be discussed in this talk.

The first is the application of numerical algorithms such as singular value decomposition, which I have been introducing in my lectures, to calculations in condensed matter physics (calculations of phonons, which are quantized crystal lattice vibrations; phonons are used in superconductivity and thermophysics). Phonons are closely related to superconductivity and thermoelectric effect.) The second is how numerical algorithms can be used in calculations as logic circuits.

The second is about the education of computer systems, including how to teach the technology of constructing a computer as a logic circuit. I will talk about how we have been able to experience the difference between logic signals as concepts and logic signals as entities, and how we have mastered the hardware description language, and what issues remain to be solved.
